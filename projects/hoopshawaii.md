During my first college semester, my friends and I would go to the Warrior Rec Center to play basketball every Friday. Ever since then, my obsession with playing basketball has only ever grown. My friends and I would choose from three courts to play with and it was usually picked out of convenience. If we were all at the University, we would just go to the Warrior Rec Center. If we were at home, like on the weekends, we would go to one of two courts depending on who's coming and what the conditions of the courts were looking like.

These were great times, but I noticed that we never really venture out to any other court on the island. When we went to these courts, they were usually empty, and sometimes we get kind of tired just playing within our group. I mean, what practice could we get if we just play the same people. We also had to move locations based on the courts or the surrounding area. Sometimes they would be undder construction, or there would be a local AAU team practicing there, already reserving the spot.

I was getting kind of tired of having to second guess myself all the time. That is when I thought of making Hoops Hawai'i for our ICS 314 Final Project. This website would allow for hoopers all around the island to find other hoopers and friends to play with.

This was the first ever time I made a full-scale website, and luckily I didn't do it all alone. Since it was a project, the instructors allowed for teams to be made. Atleast now, I wasn't doing it all alone and blind, but I had a couple of peers that I could go to for when I need assistance. To say the least, it was a great experience. I got to learn so much while having a lot of fun making a website about something that I have a passion for, basketball. The people I worked with were great. The late night sessions of stressing over random bugs and the loud cheers for when we finally fix those bugs are unforgettable.

During this project, I was responsible for a lot of the changes to the UI. Earlier this semester, I think I had the hardest time with UI, but within this project, I found it really enjoyable. The landing page, "My Courts", "Find Courts", the "Profile" card, and the "Look For Team" card was my doing and I am very proud of the product I created. I was also responsible with the backend of the "My Courts" and "Find Courts" pages. Here's where a lot of the trouble came and where I had to call upon my teammates the most. A lot of debugging had to be done here to make sure that all the right information was called. All in all, I am very happy with how the project turned out and I want to make even more changes during the summer to get it to match my true vision.
